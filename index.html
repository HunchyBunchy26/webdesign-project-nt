Places to Visit

The city's most important landmark is Daugavpils Fortress, a vast 19th-century military complex and the only preserved fortress of its kind in Eastern Europe.
Daugavpils is also a gateway to the natural landscapes of Latgale, a region known for its lakes, forests and traditional villages.

Interesting Facts about Latvia...

  • A Latvian born tailor named Jacob Davis co-invented blue jeans alongside Levi Strauss in 1873;
  • Latvian is one of the oldest living Indo-European languages in the world, with origins dating back over 5,000 years;
  • The very first documented decorated public Christmas tree was erected in Riga in 1510 by the House of the Blackheads guild;
  • Latvia is home to the Venta Rapid (Ventas Rumba) in Kuldīga, which is the widest natural waterfall in Europe, spanning an impressive 249 meters across;
  • The inspiration behind the movie Crocodile Dundee was Arvids Blumentals, a Latvian crocodile hunter who emigrated to Australia and reportedly hunted over 10,000 crocodiles;
  • Latvia has one of the worlds tallest women, with the average lady coming in at an impressive 170 cm;
  • The Baltic Sea coast in Latvia stretches for 500 kilometres and over half Latvian territory is covered by forest;
